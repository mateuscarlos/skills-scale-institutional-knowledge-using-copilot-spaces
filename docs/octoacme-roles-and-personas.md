## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile ceremonies, remove impediments, and coach the team on agile practices. They ensure the team follows agreed processes and continuously improves their ways of working.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate sprint planning, standups, reviews, and retrospectives
- Remove blockers and impediments for the team
- Coach team members on agile principles and practices
- Shield the team from external distractions and scope creep
- Track team velocity and help with capacity planning
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement and psychological safety

### Goals
- Maximize team productivity and flow
- Reduce cycle time and eliminate waste
- Build self-organizing, high-performing teams
- Ensure sustainable pace and prevent burnout

### Typical Communication & Interactions
- Daily standups with developers and cross-functional team members
- Weekly sync with Project Manager on risks, blockers, and timeline adjustments
- Regular 1:1s with team members to identify impediments and growth opportunities
- Coordinate with Product Manager on backlog refinement and sprint goals
- Escalate organizational impediments to Project Manager or leadership
- Share team velocity and metrics with stakeholders

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user-centered designs that balance user needs, business goals, and technical constraints. They conduct research, create prototypes, and validate design solutions through testing.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Design user flows and information architecture
- Collaborate with Product Manager to validate solutions meet user needs
- Partner with Developers to ensure design feasibility and quality implementation
- Maintain design systems and style guides
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and increase satisfaction
- Validate design decisions with data and user feedback
- Maintain design consistency across products

### Typical Communication & Interactions
- Design reviews with Product Manager and stakeholders
- Collaboration sessions with Developers on implementation feasibility
- User research synthesis shared with Product Manager and Project Manager
- Design handoff meetings with Developers, including specs and assets
- Participate in sprint planning to estimate design work
- Present prototypes at sprint demos and gather feedback
- Sync with Business Analyst on user requirements and workflows

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business stakeholders and technical teams by gathering requirements, documenting processes, and ensuring solutions deliver business value. They translate business needs into clear, actionable requirements.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements
- Create process flows, user stories, and acceptance criteria
- Analyze business processes and identify improvement opportunities
- Facilitate requirements workshops with stakeholders
- Validate that delivered solutions meet business needs
- Support testing by defining test scenarios and edge cases
- Track benefits realization and ROI

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete requirements that minimize rework
- Bridge communication between business and technical teams
- Maximize business value and ROI from projects
- Identify process improvements and efficiencies

### Typical Communication & Interactions
- Requirements workshops with business stakeholders and Product Manager
- Collaborate with Project Manager on scope, timeline, and dependencies
- Refinement sessions with Developers to clarify requirements and edge cases
- Work with UX Designer to ensure designs align with business processes
- Coordinate with QA on test scenarios and acceptance criteria
- Status updates to stakeholders on requirements coverage
- Support DevOps Engineer with release criteria and validation steps

---

## DevOps Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers build and maintain CI/CD pipelines, infrastructure, and monitoring systems that enable rapid, reliable software delivery. They bridge development and operations through automation and observability.

### Responsibilities
- Design and maintain CI/CD pipelines and automation
- Manage infrastructure, environments, and deployment processes
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Ensure security scanning and compliance in deployment pipelines
- Troubleshoot production issues and performance bottlenecks
- Document runbooks and incident response procedures
- Optimize deployment speed, reliability, and rollback capabilities

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe deployments with minimal downtime
- Improve system reliability, performance, and observability
- Automate repetitive tasks and reduce manual toil
- Maintain secure, scalable infrastructure

### Typical Communication & Interactions
- Collaborate with Developers on CI/CD requirements and build processes
- Work with Project Manager on deployment schedules and release readiness
- Partner with QA on test environment setup and test automation infrastructure
- Coordinate with Business Analyst and Product Manager on production validation criteria
- Incident response coordination with on-call Developers and Project Manager
- Share deployment metrics and system health dashboards with team
- Post-deployment reviews and retrospectives on release process
